Homemade Lasagna



Description

Lasagna is a timeless Italian dish that originated in the Middle Ages, with the earliest recorded recipe dating back to the 14th century. It is a a wide, flat sheet of pasta.

Lasagna can refer to either the type of noodle or to the typical lasagna dish which is a dish made with several layers of lasagna sheets with sauce and other ingredients, such as meats and cheese, in between the lasagna noodles.


Ingredients

  • Meat: Either sweet italian sausage, or lean ground beef (you can use both if you'd like).
  • Onion and Garlic: Cook one whole onion and two cloves of garlic with meat.
  • Tomato Products: You'll need one can of crushed tomatoes, two cans of tomato sauce, and two cans of tomato paste.
  • Spices and Seasonings: Flavor with fresh parsley, dried basil leaves, salt, Italian seasoning, fennel seeds, and black pepper.
  • Lasagna Noodles: Use store bought or homemade lasagna noodles.
  • Cheeses: Parmesan, mozzarella, and ricotta cheese make this lasagna extra decadent.
  • Egg: An egg helps bind the ricotta so it doesn't ooze out of the lasagna when you cut into it.

How to Layer Lasagna

Here is a brief step-by-step overview on how to layer the lasagna:

  1. Meat sauce
  2. Noodles
  3. Ricotta mixture
  4. Mozzarella slices
  5. Meat sauce
  6. Parmesan cheese
  7. Repeat the layers, then top with the remaining Parmesan

How to Prepare Lasagna

Here is a brief step-by-step overview on how to prepare the lasagna dish:

  1. Make the meat sauce.
  2. Cook the noodles.
  3. Make the ricotta mixture.
  4. Layer the lasagna according to the recipe instructions.
  5. Cover with foil and bake.
  6. Let the lasagna rest before serving.
  7. Enjoy!
